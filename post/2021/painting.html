Painting

2021–11–03 ~1000 words

A painting has two major aspects to it, the artistic and the craftsmanship dimension.[1] Whereas the proficiency of the respective artwork is a fairly quantifiable measure, artistic value is a product of personal preference and the societal system of values forming one’s personal viewpoint.

The Value of Painting

Authentic Art opposes norms and introduces a transcendental perspective, a line of thought that could be taken to reveal the lag of value in the uniform output of the culture industry [Kulturindustrie].

“Standardisierten und identischen Waren ausgeliefert, entwickeln die Konsumenten die vom System „gewünschten“ Bedürfnisse.” — [2]

The capitalist dogma of profit maximization compels the industry to deliver addicting, hence instantaneously pleasurable products to the widest audience possible, rendering them docile as the desired is repeatedly exposed, mutilating one’s capacity to value and create non-standartized art.

“Kulturindustrie sublimiert nicht sondern unterdrückt.” — [3]

Sublimation would indeed be difficult to achieve; qua establishment of a noise floor for the psychological drives, exposure to culture industrial goods should render ex nihilo creation of art impossible. Through capitalism’s ability to accommodate various forms of resistance, opposition to the mainstream only validates the status quo, makes authentic art an accomplice chained to what it negates.

“Sometimes doing nothing is the most violent thing to do” — [4], [5]

Reproductions of master’s works can have meaning as a comment on the commodification of art itself, combining craftsmanship with the distinct minimization of personal artistic involvement by the artist.

Romanticized views attribute art the function of a deeper form of communication; a special form of expression by the painter. Painting can certainly behave like diary writing, the language used however is your very own, not to be taken verbatim, as all meaning and value is derived from the viewer’s perspective. Thus, a reproduction or copy of a painting can have the same perceived value as an authentic artwork, even if the value system from where it is judged is flawed.

“Painting is just another form of keeping a diary” — [6]

My Painting

With the distinctions made ex supra,1 I am content with the fact, that this painting inheres a lower artistic value and meaning for me than some of my other paintings. Maybe take it as a comment on Kulturindustie, or something. This is probably also the reason I dislike sharing this work with others less, it just does not feel as personal/private as some others do.

The craftsmanship, with this painting only having $40\text{cm}\times30\text{cm}$ measurements and the degree of detail, proportions and reasonability of colors given, is pleasing. The paint layer is very thin and comprises of a acrylic underlayer based on the underdrawing, plotting out the different areas of similar scenery with a rough average color. Then introducing the full spectrum of detail in the second layer, and adjusting and highlighting further in the third. Factors that lead the uppermost paint layer to retain wet paint characteristics for longer, should increase in subsequent layers. This rule in mind, I increased the oil amount of the paint accordingly.

The biggest technical mistake I made with this painting, is not oiling out the jawline before reworking the area at one point. The strands of hair overlapping with the sky have a rugged texture; because I do not use any solvents as the core feature is quick dissipation making ventilation or respiratory mask use necessary, the paint’s plasticity is rather stiff. Only after varnishing did I notice, that the left eye highlight seems somewhat washed out and that the introduction of dust into the paint and varnish layer is minimal, but more than i would have liked.

Photographing the painting was a challenge in itself and I am still on the lookout for possible optimizations in the process. After varnishing the painting with Gamvar, every bit of impasto refracts and reflects the light. This image is the closest representation for now, it differs somewhat when seen tête-à-tête.
